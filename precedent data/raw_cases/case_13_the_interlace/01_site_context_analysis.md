## 1. Urban Location & Positioning

The Interlace is located in Singapore along Depot Road and Alexandra Road, near the Southern Ridges green belt. The project occupies a large elevated site within a context that combines residential neighborhoods, expressway infrastructure, green landscape, and recreational park systems.

Unlike many high-rise residential projects in Singapore that use isolated point towers, The Interlace reorganizes residential density into a horizontal and interlocking system of stacked blocks. The project can be understood as a critique of the conventional vertical tower typology.

The site can be understood through four urban conditions:

1. **Green belt edge condition**
   The project is located near the Southern Ridges, a major green corridor connecting Kent Ridge, Telok Blangah Hill, and Mount Faber. This gives the project a strong relationship with landscape, greenery, and recreational open space.

2. **Infrastructure edge condition**
   The site is close to Alexandra Road and the Ayer Rajah Expressway. This creates exposure to traffic, noise, movement, and large-scale urban infrastructure.

3. **Residential district condition**
   The surrounding area includes residential estates, condominiums, local streets, and neighborhood facilities. The project therefore needs to function as a large residential community within an existing urban district.

4. **Large-site development condition**
   The project sits on an approximately eight-hectare site, allowing a different kind of housing organization from a compact tower-and-podium model. The site area makes it possible to create courtyards, landscapes, gardens, pools, and communal open spaces at multiple levels.

The Interlace therefore operates as a large-scale residential landscape rather than a conventional tower. It uses horizontal stacking, courtyards, terraces, gardens, and shared amenities to create a vertical village embedded in Singapore’s tropical landscape.

This internal landscape strategy is important because the project is very large. Instead of creating one central open space only at the ground level, it distributes communal landscape across courtyards, roofs, terraces, and spaces between stacked blocks.

## 4. Sunlight & Shadow Conditions

Singapore has a hot and humid tropical climate with strong sunlight, high rainfall, and high humidity. Residential architecture in this climate needs shade, airflow, rain protection, usable outdoor space, and relief from heat.

The Interlace responds to this condition through its unusual massing strategy.

Key sunlight and shadow implications include:

1. **Permeable courtyard structure**
   The stacked blocks are arranged around large open courtyards. These courtyards allow daylight, air movement, and views through the complex.

2. **Horizontal block orientation**
   Instead of a field of point towers, the building uses horizontal slabs stacked at different levels. This creates shaded spaces below blocks and terraces above them.

3. **Open-air communal areas**
   Courtyards, pools, gardens, terraces, and roof decks create outdoor communal environments suitable for tropical living.

4. **Wind corridors and evaporative cooling**
   Open courtyards and water bodies can support airflow and cooling across the landscape.

5. **Shaded interstitial spaces**
   The overlapping blocks create sheltered outdoor zones that are protected from direct sun and tropical rain.

The project therefore treats massing as a climatic device. The arrangement of blocks creates shade, ventilation, open landscape, and multiple environmental conditions within the housing complex.

## 5. Views & Vistas

The Interlace produces several types of views:

### 5.1 Courtyard View

Many apartments look into the large internal courtyards, pools, gardens, and communal spaces. This creates a strong internal neighborhood identity.

### 5.2 Landscape View

Apartments and terraces facing outward can view the Southern Ridges green belt and surrounding landscape.

### 5.3 City View

Upper blocks and roof terraces can look outward toward Singapore’s cityscape, infrastructure, and surrounding districts.

### 5.4 Cross-block View

Because the blocks are stacked and interlaced, residents experience views across voids, terraces, bridges, gardens, and neighboring blocks.

The view strategy is not based only on height. It is also based on horizontal layering. Residents experience both outward landscape views and inward community views.

## 6. Design Implications

The site context suggests that The Interlace’s design strategy can be interpreted through six main ideas.

### 6.1 Replacing the isolated tower with a horizontal village

The project challenges Singapore’s common tower typology by turning vertical housing into a horizontal network of stacked blocks.

### 6.2 Creating multiple courtyards instead of one central void

The eight courtyards distribute landscape, light, air, and community across the large site.

### 6.3 Connecting housing with tropical landscape

Gardens, pools, green roofs, terraces, and open-air spaces create a residential environment suited to Singapore’s climate.

### 6.4 Creating social infrastructure through stacking

The overlapping blocks create many shared thresholds, sky terraces, roof gardens, and communal spaces.

### 6.5 Responding to infrastructure through internal landscape

Because the site is near major roads and expressway infrastructure, the project creates a protected internal landscape environment.

### 6.6 Turning density into topography

The stacked blocks create an artificial landscape of terraces, bridges, voids, and courtyards, making the building feel more like a topographic system than a typical tower.

## 7. Site Context Summary

The Interlace is located along Depot Road and Alexandra Road in Singapore, near the Southern Ridges green belt and bounded by major infrastructure and residential districts.

The main site conditions are:

1. **Large residential site**
   The eight-hectare site allows a new organizational model beyond point towers.

2. **Tropical climate**
   The project requires shade, airflow, outdoor communal space, and rain-conscious open areas.

3. **Green belt proximity**
   The Southern Ridges landscape gives the project a strong park and nature context.

4. **Infrastructure exposure**
   Alexandra Road and Ayer Rajah Expressway create traffic and noise conditions that require internal spatial buffering.

5. **Need for community at high density**
   More than one thousand units require a strong system of shared amenities, landscape, and social spaces.

The Interlace responds by stacking thirty-one six-storey apartment blocks in a hexagonal arrangement around eight large courtyards. This creates a residential environment that combines density, landscape, circulation, and community into one interlaced system.

Therefore, The Interlace can be understood as a high-rise residential precedent that transforms vertical housing into a horizontal, stacked, landscape-based village.
