## 1. Purpose of Photos and Renders Preprocess

This preprocess translates photos, diagrams, and visual materials into textual descriptions for architectural precedent analysis.

The goal is to extract:

1. **Design vocabulary**
   Visible architectural elements, stacked blocks, courtyards, roof gardens, sky gardens, and horizontal residential typology.

2. **Atmospheric description**
   The visual and spatial atmosphere created by the interlocking blocks, large courtyards, landscape, and tropical communal spaces.

3. **Design intent reading**
   The possible design intention behind replacing isolated towers with a horizontal and interwoven residential system.

4. **Contextual relationship**
   How the building relates to Singapore’s tropical climate, green belt, residential density, and communal living.

## 3. Atmospheric Description

The Interlace creates the atmosphere of a horizontal village suspended in the air.

From the exterior image, the building appears as a large composition of stacked horizontal bars. Instead of vertical towers, the project reads as a system of residential blocks woven together. The spaces between blocks are as important as the blocks themselves.

The atmosphere can be described through five qualities:

### 3.1 Horizontal Density

The project is dense, but its density is distributed horizontally rather than expressed as isolated vertical towers.

### 3.2 Interwoven Community

The stacked and overlapping blocks create a sense of interconnected community. The project appears as one large residential organism.

### 3.3 Tropical Landscape Living

Courtyards, pools, roof gardens, terraces, and planted decks create an environment suited to outdoor tropical life.

### 3.4 Topographic Architecture

The building feels like a constructed landscape. Stacked blocks form hills, terraces, plateaus, voids, and valleys.

### 3.5 Anti-Tower Identity

The visual character directly challenges the standard high-rise condominium tower. It proposes a different image of urban density.

## 5. Light and Environmental Experience

The Interlace’s environmental experience is shaped by Singapore’s tropical climate and the project’s open block arrangement.

The environmental logic includes:

1. **Courtyards bring light into the complex.**
   The eight large courtyards prevent the large development from becoming a solid mass.

2. **Open stacking allows air movement.**
   The spaces between blocks create opportunities for ventilation and wind paths.

3. **Water bodies support cooling.**
   Pools and water features contribute to evaporative cooling in outdoor spaces.

4. **Stacking creates shade.**
   Blocks overhanging and bridging over spaces create shaded outdoor areas.

5. **Roof gardens multiply green space.**
   The upper surfaces of blocks become gardens, terraces, and outdoor communal zones.

The project therefore uses massing, landscape, and water to create a tropical residential environment.

## 6. Spatial Hierarchy

The photos and diagrams suggest a clear spatial hierarchy:

### 6.1 Green Belt Layer

The project is connected to the larger Southern Ridges landscape system.

### 6.2 Site Landscape Layer

The ground plane contains gardens, courtyards, pools, and public / communal open spaces.

### 6.3 Block Layer

Six-storey apartment blocks are the main repeating building units.

### 6.4 Superlevel Layer

Blocks are stacked into larger superlevels, creating a stepped residential topography.

### 6.5 Roof Garden Layer

The tops of blocks become gardens, terraces, and outdoor spaces.

### 6.6 Community Network Layer

Courtyards, amenities, paths, terraces, and cores connect the whole complex into a vertical village.

The hierarchy can be summarized as:

**Green Belt → Site Landscape → Six-Storey Blocks → Superlevels → Roof Gardens → Vertical Village Network**

## 7. Design Intent Reading

From the exterior image, plan, unit diagrams, and concept diagram, The Interlace can be interpreted as a project that redefines dense residential living in the tropics.

The design intent can be understood in six main ways:

### 7.1 Replacing towers with stacked horizontal blocks

The project shifts density from isolated point towers into a connected network of horizontal residential bars.

### 7.2 Creating a vertical village

The interlocking blocks create multiple levels of communal space, courtyards, terraces, and amenities.

### 7.3 Multiplying ground plane

Roof gardens and terraces extend landscape upward, multiplying usable open space.

### 7.4 Supporting tropical outdoor life

Courtyards, shade, water, and vegetation create outdoor spaces suited to Singapore’s climate.

### 7.5 Creating diverse residential types

The stacking system allows multiple unit types, including simplex, duplex, townhouse, penthouse, and garden units.

### 7.6 Challenging standard condominium typology

The project offers an alternative to the typical high-rise condominium of isolated towers on a podium.

## 8. Contextual Relationship

The Interlace relates to its context in four main ways:

### 8.1 Relationship with Southern Ridges

The project connects to Singapore’s green belt and extends the landscape into the residential development.

### 8.2 Relationship with tropical climate

The open courtyards, shading, water bodies, and green roofs respond to heat, humidity, and outdoor living culture.

### 8.3 Relationship with high-density housing

The project provides over one thousand units while avoiding the default isolated tower model.

### 8.4 Relationship with infrastructure

The large internal courtyards and landscape system buffer residents from surrounding roads and expressway conditions.

## 10. Preprocess Output Summary

### Main Visual Character

The Interlace presents high-density housing as a horizontal and interlocking system of stacked residential blocks. Its identity is defined by hexagonal courtyards, roof gardens, shared landscapes, and an anti-tower massing strategy.

### Main Atmospheric Quality

The project creates an atmosphere of tropical communal living. It feels like a large residential village made of courtyards, terraces, gardens, pools, and stacked neighborhoods.

### Main Design Reading

The Interlace uses horizontal stacking to transform high-density housing into a vertical village. Its key contribution is the replacement of isolated point towers with interlocking blocks that create courtyards, landscape, community, and climatic comfort.
