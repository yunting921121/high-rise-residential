## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings and diagrams into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, residential components, terrace types, pergolas, public programs, circulation systems, and environmental devices.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How apartments, terraces, pergolas, central cores, public programs, rooftop functions, vegetation, views, privacy, and facade layers relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind L'Arbre Blanc as a branching residential tower where balconies function like tree branches extending toward light, views, and outdoor life.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Five Principles Diagram

The five principles diagram explains the project through several key ideas:

1. Inside and outside
2. Layers and terraces
3. Vegetation and architecture
4. Views and privacy
5. Light and identity

This diagram shows that the tower is organized around the relationship between interior domestic space and exterior terrace life. The balconies are not secondary add-ons; they are central to the architectural concept.

### 2.2 Tenth Floor Plan

The tenth floor plan shows a compact central elevator and stair core surrounded by apartment units. Each unit is organized around living area, dining area, kitchen, bedrooms, and terraces.

The plan also shows terraces distributed around the perimeter. Many terraces are cantilevered outward from the main tower body, creating a branching effect. The terraces connect directly to residential rooms and become outdoor extensions of the apartments.

### 2.3 Exploded Axonometric Program Drawing

The exploded axonometric drawing shows the vertical program organization of the building.

The lower levels contain public or semi-public programs such as gallery, restaurant, offices, and entrance areas. The middle and upper levels contain residential apartments with terraces. The top levels contain common space and rooftop bar / panoramic public space.

This drawing shows that L'Arbre Blanc is not only a private residential tower. It contains a vertical mixture of public, semi-public, and private programs.

### 2.4 Exterior Render

The exterior render shows the building as a white vertical trunk with balconies extending outward like branches or leaves. Pergolas and terraces appear in different directions and at different heights, creating a dynamic and irregular facade.

The image confirms that the facade is not a flat envelope. It is an inhabited zone made of outdoor spaces, sunshades, and viewing platforms.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Central Core → serves → Apartment Units

The central elevator and stair core organizes vertical circulation and distributes access to apartment units.

### 5.2 Apartment Unit → opens to → Terrace

Residential units connect directly to exterior terraces, allowing indoor rooms to extend outward.

### 5.3 Terrace → extends → Living Area

The terrace functions as an outdoor extension of the living area and creates a larger domestic environment.

### 5.4 Bedroom → connects to → Terrace

Some bedrooms are directly related to terraces, allowing private rooms to access outdoor space, light, and views.

### 5.5 Pergola → shades → Terrace

Pergolas provide shade for the terraces and help make outdoor spaces usable in Montpellier’s sunny climate.

### 5.6 Branching Balcony → projects from → Tower Trunk

Balconies project outward from the central tower body like branches from a tree.

### 5.7 Vegetation → softens → Terrace Edge

Planters and greenery soften the terrace edge and support a more garden-like outdoor living atmosphere.

### 5.8 Terrace → frames → City and River View

The terraces act as viewing platforms toward Montpellier, the Lez River, and the surrounding landscape.

### 5.9 Rooftop Bar → opens to → Public View

The rooftop bar and panoramic terrace allow visitors to access high-level views, making the tower partially public.

### 5.10 Ground Floor Restaurant → activates → Street / Plaza

The restaurant and ground-level public program connect the tower to the city and create activity at the base.

### 5.11 Mixed-use Stack → combines → Public and Private Programs

The tower combines restaurant, gallery, offices, housing, common room, and rooftop bar in one vertical structure.

### 5.12 Balconies and Pergolas → create → Light and Identity

The repeated outward terraces and pergolas define the building’s visual identity through shadow, white structure, and branching geometry.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

L'Arbre Blanc’s spatial logic is based on the idea of a tree.

The tower body functions like a trunk. Apartments are organized around the central core and tower perimeter. Terraces and balconies project outward from this trunk like branches. Pergolas and sunshades act like leaves that filter sunlight and create shade.

The residential sequence can be summarized as:

**Central Core → Apartment Unit → Living / Bedroom Space → Terrace → Pergola / Shade → City or River View**

The vertical program sequence can be summarized as:

**Ground Public Program → Residential Apartments → Common Space → Rooftop Bar / Panoramic View**

The environmental sequence can be summarized as:

**Mediterranean Sun → Pergola / Balcony Shade → Outdoor Living → Indoor Comfort**

Through this strategy, L'Arbre Blanc combines:

1. **Private residential life**
2. **Outdoor Mediterranean living**
3. **Public rooftop experience**
4. **River and city views**
5. **Tree-like architectural identity**
6. **Facade as inhabitable space**

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that L'Arbre Blanc is a project about turning balconies into the main generator of the tower.

In many high-rise residential buildings, balconies are small additions to a repetitive facade. In L'Arbre Blanc, the balcony becomes the primary spatial and symbolic element.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The balcony becomes a branch.**

The branching balcony is not only a metaphor. It performs several roles:

* It extends the apartment.
* It creates outdoor living space.
* It provides shade with pergolas.
* It frames views.
* It creates privacy through distance and orientation.
* It defines the tower’s identity.
* It connects the building to Montpellier’s outdoor culture.

This makes L'Arbre Blanc a useful precedent for high-rise residential design where outdoor space, public identity, and climate-responsive living are central issues.
