## 1. Urban Location & Positioning

L'Arbre Blanc is located in Montpellier, France, near the Lez River and the Port Marianne district. The building occupies a prominent urban position between Montpellier's existing city fabric and newer development areas along the river.

The project was developed as part of Montpellier's contemporary architectural ambition to create new urban landmarks, sometimes referred to as modern “folies.” It was intended not only as a residential tower, but also as a public and civic architectural object that enriches the city’s contemporary identity.

The site can be understood through four urban conditions:

1. **River-edge condition**
   The building is located close to the Lez River. This gives it access to open views, pedestrian riverside spaces, and a strong relationship with Montpellier’s landscape infrastructure.

2. **Port Marianne development context**
   The surrounding district contains contemporary urban development, offices, housing, public spaces, and new mixed-use urban blocks.

3. **City landmark condition**
   Because of its unusual white branching form, the building acts as a recognizable landmark in Montpellier’s skyline.

4. **Public-access mixed-use condition**
   The building includes not only private housing, but also public or semi-public functions such as a restaurant, gallery, offices, common spaces, and a rooftop bar.

L'Arbre Blanc therefore operates at two scales. At the residential scale, it provides apartments with generous terraces. At the urban scale, it becomes a civic object connected to the river, public space, and the contemporary image of Montpellier.

The building’s branching balconies respond to this context by orienting apartments outward. Instead of creating an inward-facing tower, the design extends domestic life outward toward the city, river, sun, and views.

## 4. Sunlight & Shadow Conditions

Montpellier has a warm Mediterranean climate with abundant sunlight and a strong culture of outdoor living. In this context, high-rise housing benefits from shaded terraces, outdoor rooms, natural ventilation, and protection from overheating.

L'Arbre Blanc responds to these environmental conditions through its balcony and pergola system.

Key sunlight and shadow implications include:

1. **Large cantilevered balconies**
   The balconies extend outward from the tower like branches. They create outdoor living spaces while also shading lower facade areas.

2. **Pergolas and sunshades**
   Pergolas provide additional shading and help make terraces usable under strong sun.

3. **Layered exterior spaces**
   The building uses multiple depths of balconies and terraces, creating different degrees of exposure, shelter, and privacy.

4. **Orientation toward views and light**
   The tower is designed to look outward, allowing apartments to access sunlight, views, and outdoor space.

5. **Facade identity through sunlight**
   The white balcony structure catches strong Mediterranean light and creates a changing image of shadow, brightness, and depth.

The building therefore uses its balcony system as both a lifestyle device and a passive environmental strategy.

## 5. Views & Vistas

L'Arbre Blanc’s location near the Lez River and its height allow residents and visitors to experience strong views across Montpellier and the surrounding landscape.

The project creates several view conditions:

### 5.1 River View

Terraces and rooftop areas can look toward the Lez River and adjacent riverfront landscape.

### 5.2 City View

Upper floors and rooftop spaces can overlook Montpellier’s urban fabric, including Port Marianne, Antigone, and the wider city.

### 5.3 Public Rooftop View

The rooftop bar and common areas allow not only residents but also visitors to enjoy panoramic views.

### 5.4 Terrace View

Each apartment’s outdoor terrace becomes a viewing platform. The view is not only seen through windows; it is experienced from an exterior room.

The building’s view strategy is directly connected to its outward-facing form. The many projecting terraces are arranged to give apartments access to multiple directions, sunlight, and distant views.

## 6. Design Implications

The site context suggests that L'Arbre Blanc’s design strategy can be interpreted through six main ideas.

### 6.1 Turning the tower into a public landmark

The project is not only housing. It is intended as a recognizable civic object for Montpellier, connected to the city’s tradition of experimental architectural “folies.”

### 6.2 Extending domestic life outdoors

The building responds to Mediterranean outdoor culture by giving apartments generous terraces and balconies.

### 6.3 Making the facade inhabitable

The facade is not a thin envelope. It becomes a thick layer of terraces, pergolas, outdoor rooms, sunshades, and social spaces.

### 6.4 Connecting housing to river landscape

The tower’s balconies open outward toward the river and surrounding city, connecting private residences to the wider urban landscape.

### 6.5 Combining private and public programs

The building includes apartments, offices, gallery, restaurant, common space, and rooftop bar. This makes it more public than a conventional private residential tower.

### 6.6 Creating a tree-like residential typology

The building uses branching balconies as a spatial metaphor and organizational system. The tower trunk contains vertical circulation and housing, while balconies extend like branches toward light, view, and air.

## 7. Site Context Summary

L'Arbre Blanc is located in Montpellier’s Port Marianne / Lez River area, where contemporary urban development, riverfront public space, and landmark architecture intersect.

The main site conditions are:

1. **Mediterranean climate**
   Warm weather and abundant sunlight support outdoor living but require shading.

2. **River-edge location**
   The Lez River provides landscape value, views, and public pedestrian context.

3. **Contemporary development district**
   Port Marianne provides a setting for new housing, offices, public spaces, and architectural experimentation.

4. **Landmark expectation**
   The project was intended to become a bold architectural object for Montpellier.

5. **Mixed public-private program**
   The building combines private housing with public or semi-public functions such as restaurant, gallery, offices, and rooftop bar.

L'Arbre Blanc responds by transforming the high-rise residential tower into a white branching structure. Its balconies and pergolas create outdoor living spaces, shade, views, identity, and public visibility.

Therefore, L'Arbre Blanc can be understood as a high-rise residential precedent that uses branching balconies, rooftop public space, and Mediterranean outdoor living to reinterpret the residential tower as both a private home and a civic landmark.
