## 1. Purpose of Photos and Renders Preprocess

This preprocess translates photos, renders, and visual materials into textual descriptions for architectural precedent analysis.

The goal is to extract:

1. **Design vocabulary**
   Visible architectural elements, balcony forms, pergolas, public spaces, facade components, and environmental devices.

2. **Atmospheric description**
   The visual and spatial atmosphere created by the white branching balconies, outdoor terraces, rooftop space, and riverfront urban setting.

3. **Design intent reading**
   The possible design intention behind the tower’s tree-like form and outdoor living system.

4. **Contextual relationship**
   How the building relates to Montpellier’s warm climate, outdoor culture, riverfront development, and civic architectural identity.

## 3. Atmospheric Description

L'Arbre Blanc creates the atmosphere of a residential tower transformed into a branching outdoor structure.

From the exterior render, the building appears less like a conventional high-rise and more like a vertical tree. Balconies extend outward in many directions, producing a lively and irregular silhouette. The white color, thin horizontal plates, pergolas, and terraces create a light and open atmosphere.

The atmosphere can be described through five qualities:

### 3.1 Tree-like Lightness

The tower is visually organized as a trunk with branches. This gives the building a natural and organic image, even though it is a high-rise structure.

### 3.2 Outdoor Domesticity

The balconies are large enough to support furniture, plants, and daily outdoor use. They make each apartment feel connected to exterior life.

### 3.3 Public Vertical Landmark

The building is not only private housing. Its rooftop bar, gallery, restaurant, and public visibility make it a civic object for Montpellier.

### 3.4 Mediterranean Brightness

The white structure reflects strong southern light and creates a bright, airy visual character.

### 3.5 Layered Privacy

Although the building is highly open, the terraces project in different directions. This creates separation between balconies and gives each outdoor space its own orientation and privacy condition.

## 5. Light and Environmental Experience

The building responds to Mediterranean sunlight through balconies, pergolas, and shade.

The environmental logic includes:

1. **Pergolas provide sun control.**
   The pergolas filter sunlight and help make terraces usable.

2. **Balconies create outdoor rooms.**
   The large terraces extend apartments outward and support outdoor living.

3. **Projection creates shadow.**
   Cantilevered balconies shade parts of the facade and adjacent terraces.

4. **Orientation creates privacy and view variation.**
   Balconies point in different directions, reducing direct face-to-face exposure.

5. **White material amplifies light identity.**
   The white tower reflects sunlight and reinforces the building’s identity as the “White Tree.”

The facade therefore works as a climate-responsive and lifestyle-oriented system.

## 6. Spatial Hierarchy

The images and diagrams suggest a clear spatial hierarchy:

### 6.1 City / River Layer

The building is located near the Lez River and contemporary Montpellier development.

### 6.2 Public Base Layer

The ground level includes restaurant, gallery, and public interface with the city.

### 6.3 Residential Tower Layer

Apartments form the main vertical program of the building.

### 6.4 Balcony Branch Layer

Terraces project outward from the tower body, creating the tree-like appearance.

### 6.5 Pergola / Shade Layer

Pergolas and sunshades filter sunlight and define outdoor living spaces.

### 6.6 Rooftop Public Layer

The rooftop bar and terrace provide public or semi-public access to panoramic views.

## 7. Design Intent Reading

From the render, plan, and axonometric drawing, L'Arbre Blanc can be interpreted as a project that redefines the residential tower through outdoor living and public identity.

The design intent can be understood in six main ways:

### 7.1 Making balconies the main architectural element

The project turns balconies from secondary additions into the defining spatial and visual system.

### 7.2 Encouraging residents to live outside

Large terraces, pergolas, and outdoor rooms support Montpellier’s culture of outdoor living.

### 7.3 Creating a public-private tower

The building includes private apartments, public ground-level programs, and a rooftop bar, making it more civic than a standard residential tower.

### 7.4 Using tree metaphor as spatial logic

The tower body acts like a trunk, while balconies branch outward toward light, air, and views.

### 7.5 Producing identity through climate response

Shade devices, terraces, and outdoor rooms create an architectural identity rooted in climate and lifestyle.

### 7.6 Connecting residents to city and river

The projecting terraces offer views toward Montpellier, the Lez River, and surrounding landscape.

## 8. Contextual Relationship

L'Arbre Blanc relates to its context in four main ways:

### 8.1 Relationship with Montpellier’s climate

The project responds to warm weather and abundant sunlight through terraces and pergolas.

### 8.2 Relationship with outdoor culture

The building’s large balconies support a lifestyle where domestic life extends outside.

### 8.3 Relationship with the riverfront

The tower’s outward balconies and rooftop view connect the building to the Lez River and broader urban landscape.

### 8.4 Relationship with civic identity

The project acts as a contemporary landmark and public attraction, not only a private apartment tower.

## 10. Preprocess Output Summary

### Main Visual Character

L'Arbre Blanc presents the residential tower as a white tree. Its identity is defined by branching cantilevered balconies, pergolas, terraces, rooftop public space, and bright Mediterranean light.

### Main Atmospheric Quality

The project creates an atmosphere of outdoor domestic life. It feels open, bright, playful, civic, and strongly connected to Montpellier’s climate and riverfront context.

### Main Design Reading

L'Arbre Blanc uses balconies as the main architectural system. The balconies extend living spaces, provide shade, frame views, create privacy, and transform the tower into a civic landmark.
