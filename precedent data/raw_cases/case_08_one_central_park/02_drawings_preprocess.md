## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings and diagrams into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, residential components, tower forms, podium functions, heliostat devices, green facade elements, and environmental systems.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How residential units, towers, voids, heliostat, mirror panels, podium, green walls, sky garden, and public spaces relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind One Central Park as a mixed-use tower system that combines vertical gardens, retail podium, high-rise living, and sunlight redirection technology.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 East and West Tower Floor Plans

The floor plan diagram shows two residential towers: East Tower and West Tower. Each tower contains multiple apartment units organized around vertical cores, corridors, and service zones.

The plans also show voids and special panel zones associated with the heliostat and reflector system. These voids are not ordinary leftover spaces. They are part of the environmental strategy that allows sunlight to be captured and redirected into shaded urban areas.

### 2.2 Section with Heliostat System

The section drawing shows the relationship between the two towers, podium, vertical circulation, heliostat mirrors, and reflected sunlight paths.

The heliostat system captures sunlight and redirects it toward the cantilevered reflector structure. The red light paths in the section reveal that the building is actively manipulating sunlight across multiple levels.

This makes the section important because it shows that One Central Park is not only a vertical garden building. It is also a light-distribution machine.

### 2.3 Exterior Photo

The exterior photo shows the towers rising above a podium and covered with vertical garden elements. The facade combines glass, planting, balconies, and structural frames. The large cantilevered heliostat structure projects from the taller tower and becomes a major visual identity.

The exterior confirms that the building’s environmental systems are visually expressed rather than hidden.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Residential Tower → rises above → Retail Podium

The residential towers are stacked above a multi-level retail podium, combining housing with public commercial activity at the base.

### 5.2 Retail Podium → activates → Broadway and Precinct

The podium connects the towers to street life, shopping, food, recreation, and public circulation.

### 5.3 Apartment Units → organize around → Vertical Core

Residential units are arranged around core and corridor systems, allowing repeated high-rise residential floors.

### 5.4 Vertical Garden → covers → Tower Facade

Plants and green wall systems cover large portions of the facade, creating a living skin around the residential towers.

### 5.5 Vegetation → shades → Apartment Units

Facade planting filters sunlight and provides shade to apartments, balconies, and glazing.

### 5.6 Heliostat → redirects → Sunlight

The heliostat captures and redirects sunlight toward shaded areas of the building and public realm.

### 5.7 Reflector Panels → distribute → Light

Reflective panels mounted on the cantilevered structure distribute sunlight into the retail atrium, landscaped terraces, and shaded podium areas.

### 5.8 Cantilevered Frame → supports → Reflector Panels

The dramatic cantilevered structure holds the reflector panel system and becomes part of the building’s visual identity.

### 5.9 Sky Garden → occupies → Cantilevered Level

The cantilever also supports sky garden or communal terrace functions, connecting environmental technology with shared space.

### 5.10 Void → receives → Reflected Light

The void between tower elements receives reflected light from the heliostat system, reducing the darkness of shaded internal areas.

### 5.11 Chippendale Green → connects to → Vertical Garden

The ground-level landscape concept continues upward through the tower’s vertical planting.

### 5.12 Environmental Systems → define → Building Identity

The vertical gardens and heliostat are not hidden technical systems; they form the recognizable image of One Central Park.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

One Central Park’s spatial logic is based on the integration of greenery, light, housing, and urban renewal.

The vertical spatial sequence can be summarized as:

**Retail Podium → Residential Towers → Vertical Gardens → Heliostat / Sky Garden → Reflected Light**

The environmental sequence can be summarized as:

**Sunlight → Heliostat → Reflector Panels → Atrium / Podium / Terraces → Public Realm**

The landscape sequence can be summarized as:

**Chippendale Green → Podium Landscape → Vertical Garden → Sky Garden**

Through this strategy, One Central Park combines:

1. **High-rise residential density**
2. **Retail and public podium life**
3. **Vertical garden facade**
4. **Daylight redirection technology**
5. **Urban renewal of a former industrial site**
6. **Green landmark identity**

The building is therefore both a residential tower and an environmental device.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that One Central Park is a project about making environmental systems visible and spatially active.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The facade becomes an environmental interface.**

The facade does not only separate inside and outside. It supports vegetation, shade, view, identity, and ecological expression. At the same time, the heliostat system treats light as an architectural resource that can be redirected to improve public and interior space.

One Central Park is therefore useful as a precedent for high-rise housing where green facade, public podium, and environmental technology are integrated into one design system.
