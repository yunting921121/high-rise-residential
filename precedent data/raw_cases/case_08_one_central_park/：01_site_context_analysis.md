## 1. Urban Location & Positioning

One Central Park is located at 28 Broadway in Chippendale, Sydney. It is part of the larger Central Park Sydney urban renewal development on the former Carlton & United Brewery site.

The project occupies a strategic position along Broadway, one of Sydney’s important urban corridors. It is located near the University of Technology Sydney, Central Station, Chippendale’s fine-grain urban fabric, and the transition between Sydney’s central business district and inner-city residential neighborhoods.

The site can be understood through four urban conditions:

1. **Former industrial redevelopment site**
   The project is part of the transformation of a former brewery site into a mixed-use precinct containing housing, retail, student living, offices, hotels, public open space, and landscape.

2. **Broadway urban gateway condition**
   The site sits along Broadway, a major street corridor that connects Sydney’s CBD, Chippendale, Ultimo, and university district.

3. **High-density mixed-use precinct**
   One Central Park combines residential towers, serviced apartments, retail podium, green walls, public space, and environmental systems within one development.

4. **Urban park and green infrastructure condition**
   The project is closely connected to Chippendale Green and the Central Park precinct’s landscape strategy. The building extends greenery vertically onto the tower facade.

One Central Park therefore functions as both a residential tower and an urban environmental infrastructure. It is not only a high-rise building; it is a mixed-use urban renewal device that combines housing, retail, landscape, and sunlight technologies.

## 4. Sunlight & Shadow Conditions

The project’s environmental design is strongly shaped by the need to bring light into dense urban spaces.

One Central Park uses a heliostat system to capture sunlight and redirect it into areas that would otherwise be shaded by the towers. The heliostat is positioned above the lower tower and connected to a large cantilevered reflector structure on the taller tower.

This system creates several environmental implications:

1. **Light redirection into shaded areas**
   The heliostat redirects sunlight into the retail atrium, landscaped terraces, and public areas.

2. **Response to high-density urban shadow**
   Because high-rise towers can cast shadows on podium spaces and parks, the heliostat compensates by redirecting light.

3. **Creation of artificial dappled light**
   The reflected light creates a filtered effect, similar to sunlight passing through a tree canopy.

4. **Integration of technology and architecture**
   The heliostat is not hidden. It becomes a visible architectural element and part of the building’s identity.

5. **Support for planting and public space**
   By increasing light in shaded areas, the system supports landscape, retail, and pedestrian experience.

The building therefore treats sunlight as a design material. Light is captured, redirected, and redistributed as part of the architecture.

## 5. Views & Vistas

One Central Park creates several view conditions for residents, visitors, and the city.

### 5.1 City View

The upper residential units look toward Sydney’s urban skyline, Broadway corridor, and surrounding inner-city districts.

### 5.2 Park View

Units and public spaces facing inward can look toward Chippendale Green and the landscaped Central Park precinct.

### 5.3 Vertical Garden View

The immediate foreground of many units is not only the city, but also vertical planting, green walls, balcony planting, and facade vegetation.

### 5.4 Heliostat / Sky Garden View

The cantilevered heliostat and sky garden create a recognizable visual identity. The building is viewed not only as a tower, but as an environmental machine that reflects light and supports greenery.

The view strategy is therefore both outward and inward. Residents can look to the city and park, while the city views the building as a green landmark along Broadway.

## 6. Design Implications

The site context suggests that One Central Park’s design strategy can be interpreted through six main ideas.

### 6.1 Transforming a former industrial site into a green mixed-use precinct

The project is part of a larger urban renewal process, turning the former brewery site into a dense mixed-use neighborhood.

### 6.2 Extending landscape vertically

Instead of limiting landscape to the ground plane, the project brings greenery upward through planted facades and vertical gardens.

### 6.3 Using technology to compensate for urban shadow

The heliostat system redirects sunlight into areas affected by high-rise shadows, making light a controllable environmental resource.

### 6.4 Combining residential towers with retail podium

The project integrates high-rise housing above a multi-level retail and recreation podium, creating daily urban activity at the base.

### 6.5 Creating a green gateway to downtown Sydney

The building’s vegetation and heliostat create a strong visual identity along Broadway and act as a landmark for the Central Park precinct.

### 6.6 Connecting sustainability with image

The building’s environmental systems are not hidden. Green walls, heliostat mirrors, and landscape become part of the architectural expression.

## 7. Site Context Summary

One Central Park is located at 28 Broadway, Chippendale, within the Central Park Sydney precinct on the former Carlton & United Brewery site.

The main site conditions are:

1. **Urban renewal context**
   The project transforms an industrial site into a dense mixed-use residential, retail, and landscape precinct.

2. **Broadway frontage**
   The building faces a major urban corridor and acts as a gateway to the precinct.

3. **High-density shadow condition**
   Dense towers and podium spaces require strategies for daylight, reflection, and public-space comfort.

4. **Landscape integration**
   Chippendale Green and the vertical gardens create a strong relationship between ground landscape and tower facade.

5. **Mixed-use public activity**
   The retail podium connects the tower to public urban life.

One Central Park responds by integrating residential towers, retail podium, vertical gardens, and heliostat technology into a single environmental and urban design system.

Therefore, One Central Park can be understood as a high-rise residential precedent that uses vertical greenery and sunlight redirection to connect tower living, public space, retail activity, and urban renewal.
