## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings and diagrams into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, unit modules, circulation components, outdoor spaces, facade elements, and program zones.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How apartment modules, corridors, cores, terraces, living rooms, technical floors, retail floors, and tower volumes relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind Norra Tornen as a twin-tower residential project based on modular stacking, facade depth, and apartment individuality.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Typical Floor Plan

The typical floor plan shows a compact residential tower floor organized around a central corridor and core zone. Multiple apartment units are arranged around the perimeter, and many units have access to balconies or recessed outdoor spaces.

The plan is not a simple rectangular slab. Instead, the building perimeter is irregular and pixelated. Apartment rooms project outward or pull back, creating a varied facade edge. This creates different apartment types, multiple orientations, and terrace conditions.

The floor plan also shows that the repeated module is not used to create monotony. Instead, repetition is combined with small shifts and offsets to create diversity.

### 2.2 Program Diagram

The program diagram separates the two towers, Helix and Innovationen, into vertical stacks of different program types.

The diagram shows:

* Apartments as the dominant program
* Retail at lower levels
* Lobby and circulation spaces
* Technical floors
* Service spaces
* Shafts and stairs

This reveals that the towers are primarily residential but include mixed-use and support programs at lower and intermediate levels. The diagram also shows that each tower is composed as a vertical stack of programmatic layers.

### 2.3 Exterior Photo

The exterior photo shows the relationship between the plan logic and the built facade. The pixelated edge seen in the plan becomes a three-dimensional facade of protruding boxes, recessed terraces, and repeated concrete modules.

The towers appear both monumental and domestic. They are tall urban markers, but their surfaces are composed of smaller residential-scale elements.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Twin Towers → frame → Urban Gateway

The two towers stand together as a pair and mark the entrance to the Hagastaden district.

### 5.2 Central Corridor → connects → Apartment Units

The central corridor distributes access to apartment units arranged around the tower floor.

### 5.3 Vertical Core → serves → Residential Floors

Elevators, stairs, and service shafts support vertical circulation and repeated residential floors.

### 5.4 Apartment Units → occupy → Perimeter

Apartment units are arranged around the tower perimeter so that rooms can access daylight, views, and exterior facade conditions.

### 5.5 Recessed Terrace → extends → Apartment Unit

Recessed terraces create private outdoor spaces connected to apartments.

### 5.6 Protruding Living Room → projects from → Tower Facade

Living rooms or domestic spaces project outward from the facade, producing bay-like conditions and increasing facade depth.

### 5.7 Concrete Module → forms → Pixelated Facade

Repeated prefabricated concrete modules create the tower’s pixelated and stacked appearance.

### 5.8 Alternating Void and Solid → creates → Facade Depth

The pattern of protruding rooms and recessed terraces produces a facade that is deep, shadowed, and varied.

### 5.9 Retail Base → activates → Street Level

Retail and public-facing functions at lower levels connect the towers to the surrounding urban life.

### 5.10 Technical Floors → separate → Residential Stacks

Technical floors and service zones organize building systems and divide the residential stack into programmatic segments.

### 5.11 Program Stack → organizes → Tower Function

The vertical program diagram shows apartments, retail, lobby, service, technical spaces, and shafts arranged as layers within the towers.

### 5.12 Repeated Module → allows → Apartment Variation

Although the towers use repeated modules, their arrangement creates different apartment layouts, facade positions, outdoor spaces, and views.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

Norra Tornen’s spatial logic is based on the transformation of modular repetition into residential individuality.

The project uses repeated concrete modules and apartment units, but it does not create a flat or repetitive tower surface. Instead, the modules are shifted, recessed, and projected to create a deep facade.

The main spatial sequence can be summarized as:

**Central Core / Corridor → Apartment Unit → Protruding Room or Recessed Terrace → City View**

The vertical logic can be summarized as:

**Retail / Lobby Base → Technical and Service Layers → Residential Stack → Tower Landmark**

The architectural logic can be summarized as:

**Repeated Concrete Module → Pixelated Facade → Individual Apartment Expression → Urban Landmark**

Through this strategy, Norra Tornen combines:

1. **High-rise residential density**
2. **Modular construction logic**
3. **Apartment individuality**
4. **Private outdoor space**
5. **Urban landmark presence**

The facade is not only a visual system. It is directly connected to apartment layouts, outdoor spaces, daylight, and domestic identity.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that Norra Tornen is a project about negotiating between repetition and individuality.

High-rise housing often depends on repeated units and efficient stacking. This can produce monotonous facades and anonymous living conditions. Norra Tornen uses repetition differently. The project accepts modularity but arranges it into a varied composition of projections, recesses, terraces, and apartment types.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The facade becomes a translation of domestic variety.**

Instead of hiding the repetition of residential units behind a uniform skin, the project expresses the units as stacked and shifted volumes. This creates a building that is simultaneously systematic and irregular.
