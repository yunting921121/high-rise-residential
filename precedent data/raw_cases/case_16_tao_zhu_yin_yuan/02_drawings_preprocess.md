## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, residential components, planted terraces, double-helix geometry, core systems, and environmental devices.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How the central core, paired residences, rotating terraces, planted balconies, parking system, private gardens, and sustainable systems relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind Tao Zhu Yin Yuan as a DNA-inspired residential tower where each residence becomes a sky villa with a large planted garden.

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Section Drawings

The section drawings show the tower rising above a podium and basement. The residential levels rotate gradually, creating a double-helix profile. The central vertical core remains stable while the residential wings and terraces rotate around it.

The section also shows planted terraces on both sides of the tower, rooftop garden elements, basement levels, and central circulation systems. The building is organized as a vertical stack of paired apartments, each with large exterior garden terraces.

The section is important because it shows the main design principle:

**Stable Core → Rotating Residential Wings → Planted Terraces → DNA Double Helix**

The plan shows a large luxury residential floor organized around a central core. The plan includes:

The plan indicates that each level contains two large residences arranged symmetrically around the central core. Each residence has a deep planted terrace, creating a garden-like exterior space.

### 2.3 Exterior Photo

The exterior photo shows the tower’s double-helix form clearly. The building appears as two twisting wings wrapped around a central vertical core. Large terraces project outward, and planting is distributed along the facade.

The exterior confirms that the building’s identity is generated by rotation, symmetry, terraces, and vegetation.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Central Core → anchors → Rotating Residential Wings

The central core remains vertical and stable while the apartment wings rotate around it.

### 5.2 Residential Wings → form → DNA Double Helix

The two residential wings twist around the core, creating a double-helix form inspired by DNA.

### 5.3 Floor Rotation → creates → 90-Degree Twist

Each level rotates slightly, and the accumulated rotation creates the tower’s 90-degree overall twist.

### 5.4 Two Residences → compose → One Full Floor

Each residential level is composed of two large apartment units, creating a paired organization.

### 5.5 Apartment Unit → opens to → Private Sky Garden

Each residence connects directly to a large planted terrace, turning the apartment into a sky villa.

### 5.6 Planted Terrace → extends → Living and Bedroom Areas

Living rooms, dining rooms, and bedrooms connect to the terrace, extending domestic life outward.

### 5.7 Vegetation → shades → Interior Space

Trees and shrubs on the terraces provide shade and reduce direct solar heat gain.

### 5.8 Vegetation → filters → Urban View and Privacy

Planting creates a semi-transparent buffer between private residences and the surrounding city.

### 5.9 Car Elevator → connects → Basement and Sky Garage

The car elevator system allows vehicles to move from basement parking to private sky garage access conditions.

### 5.10 Private Elevator Hall → separates → Resident Circulation

Private elevator halls support privacy and exclusivity for each residence.

### 5.11 Service Elevator → separates → Domestic Service Movement

Service elevators and back-of-house routes separate service movement from resident circulation.

### 5.12 Rooftop Energy Systems → support → Sustainable Operation

Solar panels and other rooftop technical systems contribute to the building’s environmental performance.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

Tao Zhu Yin Yuan’s spatial logic is based on the transformation of the residential floor into a rotating garden villa.

The massing sequence can be summarized as:

**Central Core → Two Residential Wings → 4.5-Degree Floor Rotation → 90-Degree Double Helix → Vertical Forest Tower**

The residential sequence can be summarized as:

**Private Elevator Arrival → Interior Living Space → Planted Terrace → Skyline View**

The environmental sequence can be summarized as:

**Vegetation → Shade → Cooling → Privacy → Carbon Absorption → Urban Ecological Image**

Through this strategy, the building combines:

1. **Luxury residential privacy**
2. **Large private sky gardens**
3. **DNA-inspired symbolic form**
4. **Carbon-absorbing vertical forest**
5. **Rotating floor plates**
6. **Private and service circulation separation**
7. **Taipei skyline landmark identity**

The project demonstrates how a residential tower can become both a private garden residence and an ecological urban symbol.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that Tao Zhu Yin Yuan is a project about turning a luxury residential tower into a vertical ecological organism.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The floor plate becomes a living helix.**

The building does not simply stack floors vertically. Each level turns slightly, creating a dynamic double-helix structure. This twist gives each residence a distinct orientation, terrace condition, garden exposure, and skyline relationship.

The building also transforms the balcony into a major ecological space. The planted terraces are not small decorative balconies. They are large garden courtyards in the sky.

This makes Tao Zhu Yin Yuan a useful precedent for high-rise housing where environmental image, private outdoor space, carbon-absorbing greenery, and iconic form are central issues.
