## 3. Atmospheric Description

Tao Zhu Yin Yuan creates the atmosphere of a futuristic ecological residence in the dense city.

From the exterior photo, the building appears as a sculptural tower twisting upward like a DNA strand. The two residential wings rotate around a central vertical core, creating a symmetrical double-helix image. Large terraces extend from the tower and are planted with trees and shrubs, making the building read as a vertical forest.

The atmosphere can be described through five qualities:

### 3.1 Futuristic Ecology

The building combines advanced technology, symbolic form, and vegetation to create a futuristic image of ecological urban living.

### 3.2 Luxury Garden Living

Each residence is not only an apartment but a large private sky villa with garden terrace, view, privacy, and separated circulation.

### 3.3 Iconic Symmetry

The twin twisting wings create a strong visual identity, making the tower stand out from conventional rectangular high-rises.

### 3.4 Urban Forest Image

The planted terraces make the tower appear like a large tree or forest organism inserted into Taipei’s dense commercial core.

### 3.5 Climate and Carbon Narrative

The project’s image is closely tied to carbon absorption, anti-global-warming messaging, and vertical greening.

## 5. Light and Environmental Experience

The building’s environmental experience is shaped by Taipei’s climate, the rotating geometry, and the planted terrace system.

The environmental logic includes:

1. **Rotation creates varied exposure.**
   Each floor turns slightly, creating different relationships to sun, shade, wind, and view.

2. **Vegetation shades the facade.**
   Trees and shrubs reduce direct sunlight and help cool terrace spaces.

3. **Terraces create thermal buffers.**
   Large garden terraces sit between interior rooms and the hot urban exterior.

4. **Rainwater supports planting.**
   Rainwater reuse and irrigation systems support the vertical forest.

5. **Energy systems support operation.**
   Solar panels and wind turbines contribute to the building’s sustainable image and performance.

6. **Planting must resist wind and typhoons.**
   Because the vegetation is placed high above ground, maintenance, wind resistance, and waterproofing are essential.

The project uses vegetation not as decoration but as an environmental and symbolic system.

### 6.1 City Layer

The tower is located in Taipei’s dense Xinyi District.

### 6.2 Site Garden Layer

The building is surrounded by planted and water-based landscape that buffers the tower from traffic and city pollution.

### 6.3 Central Core Layer

The core contains vertical circulation, elevators, staircases, services, and car access systems.

### 6.4 Paired Residence Layer

Each residential level contains two large units arranged as a pair around the core.

### 6.5 Sky Garden Layer

Each residence opens to a large planted terrace or garden courtyard.

### 6.6 Energy / Roof Layer

The top of the building contains roof garden, pergola, and renewable energy systems.

**City → Site Garden → Central Core → Paired Residences → Private Sky Gardens → Rooftop Energy / Ecological Crown**

## 7. Design Intent Reading

From the exterior photo, plan, and sections, Tao Zhu Yin Yuan can be interpreted as a project that merges luxury housing with ecological symbolism.

The design intent can be understood in six main ways:

### 7.1 Turning the apartment into a sky villa

Each residence has a large interior, private garden, separate access, and skyline view.

### 7.2 Making carbon absorption visible

The planted terraces express the project’s claim of carbon absorption and ecological responsibility.

### 7.3 Using DNA as a symbol of life

The double-helix form represents life, growth, and ecological continuity.

### 7.4 Creating a landmark for Xinyi District

The tower’s rotating form and vegetation make it visually distinct within Taipei’s skyline.

### 7.5 Combining privacy and environmental openness

Large terraces provide outdoor life while vegetation filters views and protects privacy.

### 7.6 Treating maintenance as part of design

The vertical forest requires careful irrigation, soil, stabilization, access, and long-term plant care.
