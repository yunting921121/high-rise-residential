## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings and diagrams into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, residential unit components, tower blocks, lift lobbies, sky gardens, circulation networks, communal spaces, and landscape systems.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How residential units, lift lobbies, voids, skybridges, sky gardens, central plaza, historical park, community facilities, and city context relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind The Pinnacle@Duxton as a high-density public housing project that brings landscape and community facilities into the sky.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Typical Residential Floor Plan

The typical floor plan shows multiple residential units organized around lift lobbies, escape staircases, household shelters, service areas, and voids.

Each unit includes major domestic spaces such as living room, dining room, bedrooms, kitchen, bathrooms, yard, AC ledge, bay window, planter, foyer, and household shelter.

The plan reveals a dense and efficient public housing layout. Units are arranged along the perimeter for light and ventilation, while vertical circulation and services are concentrated in shared cores.

### 2.2 Site Plan

The site plan shows seven tower blocks arranged across a long site bounded by Cantonment Road, Neil Road, Yan Kit Road, and Duxton Plain Park.

The towers are organized around central landscape spaces, historical park areas, playgrounds, food court, childcare center, event plaza, basketball court, pavilion, carpark entrances, and resident committee facilities.

The site plan reveals that the project is not simply a set of seven towers. It is a complete residential precinct with ground-level community infrastructure.

### 2.3 Sky Garden Circulation Diagram

The sky garden poster explains how circulation flows through the project at multiple levels.

It shows sky gardens at:

* 26th floor
* 50th floor

It also identifies nodal points, circulation paths, observation decks, jogging track, lounge, beach, meadow, hillock, sky gym, space net, crater, padi field, and other themed landscape zones.

This diagram is important because it shows that the sky gardens are not only viewing decks. They are designed as a continuous elevated landscape network connecting all seven towers.

### 2.4 Exterior Photo

The exterior photo shows seven tall residential blocks connected by high-level skybridges. The towers form a strong skyline and create a distinctive public housing identity.

The image confirms the main architectural strategy: separate vertical housing blocks are linked by horizontal sky gardens, producing a vertical community system.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Seven Towers → form → Residential Precinct

The seven 50-storey tower blocks form a high-density public housing precinct within central Singapore.

### 5.2 Skybridges → connect → Seven Towers

Skybridges at the 26th and 50th floors connect all seven towers into one continuous elevated network.

### 5.3 Sky Garden → extends → Communal Landscape

The sky gardens extend landscape and communal space vertically into the tower system.

### 5.4 26th Floor Sky Garden → serves → Residents

The 26th floor sky garden provides resident-oriented communal spaces, recreation, play, and social activity.

### 5.5 50th Floor Sky Garden → opens to → Public View

The 50th floor skybridge provides panoramic views and controlled public access.

### 5.6 Lift Lobby → serves → Apartment Units

Lift lobbies and vertical cores distribute access to residential units on each floor.

### 5.7 Apartment Units → occupy → Tower Perimeter

Apartment units are arranged along the tower perimeter to access daylight, air, and exterior views.

### 5.8 Household Shelter → supports → Unit Safety

Household shelters are integrated into the apartment layout as required safety spaces.

### 5.9 Central Plaza → organizes → Ground-Level Community

The central plaza and park spaces organize playgrounds, courts, food court, childcare, and community facilities.

### 5.10 Duxton Plain Park → connects to → Ground Landscape

The adjacent park landscape connects to the estate’s ground-level open spaces.

### 5.11 Jogging Track → loops through → Sky Garden

The jogging track uses the continuous skybridge to create a high-level recreational loop.

### 5.12 Nodal Points → structure → Sky Garden Circulation

Nodal points along the sky garden organize observation decks, activity zones, and movement across the seven towers.

### 5.13 Community Facilities → activate → Residential Base

Food court, childcare centre, residents’ committee spaces, and play areas create daily community life at ground level.

### 5.14 Sky Gardens → transform → Public Housing Typology

The sky gardens convert high-density public housing from a vertical stack of units into a connected vertical community.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

The Pinnacle@Duxton’s spatial logic is based on vertical connection.

The tower sequence can be summarized as:

**Seven 50-storey Towers → Lift Cores → Apartment Units → Skybridge Connections → Vertical Community**

The landscape sequence can be summarized as:

**Duxton Plain Park → Central Plaza → 26th Floor Sky Garden → 50th Floor Sky Garden → City View**

The circulation sequence can be summarized as:

**Ground-Level Community Facilities → Lift Lobby → Residential Floors → Sky Garden Network → Observation Deck**

Through this strategy, The Pinnacle@Duxton combines:

1. **High-density public housing**
2. **Efficient residential unit planning**
3. **Vertical communal landscape**
4. **Public and resident sky gardens**
5. **Ground-level community facilities**
6. **City skyline views**
7. **Tropical elevated outdoor spaces**

The project demonstrates how public housing can be both dense and communal by distributing landscape and social space vertically.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that The Pinnacle@Duxton is a project about transforming public housing into vertical urban infrastructure.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The skybridge becomes a neighborhood street in the sky.**

The skybridge is not only a circulation device. It functions as:

* a landscape path
* a jogging track
* a viewing deck
* a social condenser
* a community connector
* a recreational zone
* a public landmark
* a vertical extension of ground-level park space

This makes The Pinnacle@Duxton a useful precedent for high-rise housing where density, public access, shared landscape, and community identity must be combined.
