## 1. Purpose of Photos and Renders Preprocess

This preprocess translates photos, facade images, and visual materials into textual descriptions for architectural precedent analysis.

The goal is to extract:

1. **Design vocabulary**  
   Visible architectural elements, facade layers, winter gardens, balconies, curtains, transparency, and resident-controlled spaces.

2. **Atmospheric description**  
   The visual and spatial atmosphere created by the transformed facade, enlarged apartments, lightness, and domestic occupation.

3. **Design intent reading**  
   The possible design intention behind the non-demolition strategy and exterior extension system.

4. **Contextual relationship**  
   How the building relates to Paris social housing renewal, resident continuity, and low-carbon transformation.

## 3. Atmospheric Description

Tour Bois-le-Prêtre creates the atmosphere of an old social housing tower made generous through addition.

From the exterior photo, the transformed building appears lighter, more transparent, and more inhabited than a typical post-war concrete tower. The facade is layered with balconies, winter gardens, curtains, furniture, and daily domestic traces.

The atmosphere can be described through five qualities:

### 3.1 Generosity from Addition

The project improves quality not by replacing the building, but by adding new space to each apartment.

### 3.2 Domestic Transparency

The transparent facade reveals curtains and traces of everyday life, making the building feel inhabited and adaptable.

### 3.3 Social Housing Dignity

The transformation gives an existing public housing tower a new image without erasing its residents or structure.

### 3.4 Climate Buffer Living

The winter gardens create a soft intermediate zone between apartment and city.

### 3.5 Lightness after Modernism

The new facade layer visually lightens the older tower and updates its relationship with the city.

## 5. Light and Environmental Experience

The building’s environmental experience is shaped by the new facade depth.

The environmental logic includes:

1. **Winter gardens capture solar gain.**  
   The intermediate glazed space can warm apartments during cold seasons.

2. **Balconies provide open-air access.**  
   Residents gain exterior space previously missing from many units.

3. **Curtains allow privacy and shading.**  
   The facade becomes adjustable through resident use.

4. **Glazing increases daylight.**  
   Larger transparent surfaces improve brightness and views.

5. **Facade depth improves acoustic and thermal comfort.**  
   The added layer buffers noise, cold, heat, and urban exposure.

The project therefore uses the facade as an environmental tool and as a domestic living zone.

## 6. Spatial Hierarchy

The photos and drawings suggest a clear spatial hierarchy:

### 6.1 City / Peripheral Context Layer

The tower is located at the edge of Paris near Porte Pouchet and the ring road.

### 6.2 Existing Structure Layer

The original social housing tower is preserved as the main structural body.

### 6.3 Apartment Layer

Existing apartments remain the base units of the project.

### 6.4 Extension Layer

New floor slabs enlarge the apartments outward.

### 6.5 Winter Garden Layer

Glazed intermediate spaces create seasonal living rooms and climate buffers.

### 6.6 Balcony Layer

Open-air balconies complete the transition between home and exterior.

The hierarchy can be summarized as:

**City Edge → Existing Tower → Existing Apartment → Extension → Winter Garden → Balcony**

## 7. Design Intent Reading

From the exterior photo, facade image, and floor plan, Tour Bois-le-Prêtre can be interpreted as a project that redefines renovation as addition.

The design intent can be understood in six main ways:

### 7.1 Avoiding demolition

The project proves that an existing social housing tower can be improved rather than demolished.

### 7.2 Adding space as social value

The new extensions directly improve residents’ daily lives by making apartments larger.

### 7.3 Creating winter gardens as flexible rooms

Winter gardens function as climate buffers, sunrooms, storage, living extensions, and adaptable domestic spaces.

### 7.4 Letting residents control the facade

Curtains and sliding panels allow individual adjustment of privacy, sunlight, and ventilation.

### 7.5 Making low-carbon renovation visible

The project’s environmental value is tied to reuse, preservation, and material economy.

### 7.6 Transforming image without erasing identity

The building gains a new facade and new living quality while keeping its existing structure and social role.

## 8. Contextual Relationship

Tour Bois-le-Prêtre relates to its context in four main ways:

### 8.1 Relationship with Paris social housing

The project offers a model for improving older social housing without displacement.

### 8.2 Relationship with Porte Pouchet renewal

The tower participates in the broader transformation of a peripheral Paris neighborhood.

### 8.3 Relationship with climate and energy

The winter garden layer improves comfort and reduces dependence on purely mechanical solutions.

### 8.4 Relationship with residents

The project prioritizes resident continuity, apartment enlargement, and daily use rather than architectural image alone.

## 10. Preprocess Output Summary

### Main Visual Character

Tour Bois-le-Prêtre presents an existing social housing tower transformed through a transparent and inhabited facade layer. Its identity is defined by winter gardens, balconies, curtains, and expanded domestic space.

### Main Atmospheric Quality

The project creates an atmosphere of generosity, lightness, and social care. It makes old housing feel more open, spacious, adaptable, and dignified.

### Main Design Reading

Tour Bois-le-Prêtre uses addition rather than demolition to transform social housing. Its key contribution is the conversion of the facade into extra living space, climate buffer, and resident-controlled domestic layer.
