## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**  
   Important spatial elements, existing apartment components, new extensions, winter gardens, balconies, circulation cores, and facade systems.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**  
   How existing apartments, added floor plates, winter gardens, balconies, facade layers, stairs, elevators, and residential rooms relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**  
   The design logic behind Tour Bois-le-Prêtre as a transformation project that improves an existing social housing tower through additive space.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Typical Transformed Floor Plan

The plan shows a dense residential floor organized around central vertical circulation and repeated apartment units.

The existing core contains stairs, elevators, service areas, and shared circulation. Apartments are arranged around the perimeter. The transformation adds new exterior space along the facade, including winter gardens and balconies.

The plan reveals that the project is not a complete internal demolition. Instead, the existing apartments are enlarged through external additions.

The most important spatial change is:

**Existing Room → Winter Garden → Balcony → Exterior View**

### 2.2 Facade Image

The facade image shows a new transparent and layered envelope. Curtains, glazing, balconies, and winter garden spaces create a changing facade.

Unlike a fixed opaque facade, the new facade is affected by resident use. Curtains, furniture, plants, and personal objects appear behind the glazing, making domestic life visible.

The facade becomes more open, flexible, and inhabited.

### 2.3 Exterior Photograph

The exterior photo shows the transformed tower with new horizontal balcony slabs and transparent winter garden layers. The building becomes lighter and more generous compared with a typical post-war concrete slab.

The image confirms that the transformation is based on an added exterior structure rather than a decorative facade replacement.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Existing Tower → receives → External Extension

The existing tower is kept and expanded outward through a new self-supporting structure.

### 5.2 New Floor Plate → enlarges → Apartment Unit

Added floor slabs increase the usable space of each apartment.

### 5.3 Winter Garden → mediates → Interior and Exterior

The winter garden creates an intermediate climatic and spatial layer between the apartment and the outside.

### 5.4 Balcony → extends → Winter Garden

The balcony provides direct open-air space beyond the winter garden.

### 5.5 Existing Rooms → open to → New Facade Layer

Existing living rooms and bedrooms gain new light, view, and spatial depth through the added facade.

### 5.6 Sliding Glass Panels → allow → Climate Control

Movable glazed panels allow residents to open or close the winter garden according to season and weather.

### 5.7 Curtains → modulate → Privacy and Sunlight

Curtains and internal layers allow residents to adjust privacy, glare, and solar exposure.

### 5.8 Self-supporting Structure → avoids → Heavy Internal Demolition

The external structure allows apartment enlargement without destroying the existing tower.

### 5.9 Renovation Strategy → preserves → Resident Continuity

The project supports the possibility of residents remaining connected to their homes during transformation.

### 5.10 Facade Depth → improves → Thermal and Acoustic Comfort

The new facade layer acts as a buffer against cold, heat, wind, noise, and pollution.

### 5.11 Apartment Enlargement → improves → Social Housing Quality

The added space transforms small apartments into more generous homes.

### 5.12 Non-demolition Strategy → reduces → Waste and Carbon

Keeping the existing structure reduces demolition waste and embodied carbon compared with replacement.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

Tour Bois-le-Prêtre’s spatial logic is based on addition.

The renovation sequence can be summarized as:

**Existing Tower → External Self-supporting Structure → Added Floor Plate → Winter Garden → Balcony → New Living Quality**

The residential sequence can be summarized as:

**Existing Apartment → Enlarged Room → Winter Garden → Balcony → View / Light / Air**

The environmental sequence can be summarized as:

**Exterior Climate → Balcony → Winter Garden → Existing Interior**

Through this strategy, the project combines:

1. **Preservation of existing structure**
2. **Apartment enlargement**
3. **Winter garden climatic buffering**
4. **Balcony outdoor space**
5. **Resident continuity**
6. **Social housing improvement**
7. **Low-carbon renovation**

The project demonstrates that improving housing can be achieved by adding space rather than demolishing and rebuilding.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that Tour Bois-le-Prêtre is a project about changing the value of existing housing.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The facade becomes extra living space.**

The facade is not a thin visual surface. It becomes an inhabited layer containing winter gardens, balconies, curtains, daylight, views, and personal use.

The project also reverses the logic of demolition. Instead of considering the existing tower as obsolete, the design treats it as a structure capable of growth.

This makes Tour Bois-le-Prêtre a useful precedent for high-rise housing where the goal is to improve quality, reduce carbon, preserve residents, and add flexibility to existing buildings.
