## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, residential components, balcony types, service areas, circulation elements, and environmental devices.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How full-floor apartments, central living spaces, bedrooms, service rooms, garden balconies, vertical circulation, and facade walls relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind EDEN as a high-rise residential tower that turns each apartment into a private garden home.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Typical Residential Floor Plan

The typical floor plan shows one full-floor apartment occupying the entire floor plate. The plan is organized around a large central living and dining space, with bedrooms and service spaces distributed around the perimeter.

The plan also shows curved planted balconies placed around the edges of the apartment. These garden balconies act as extensions of the interior living spaces and create a green buffer between the apartment and the surrounding city.

The unit does not behave like a typical high-rise apartment divided into small repeated units. Instead, each floor operates more like a private house lifted into the tower.

### 2.2 Section Drawing

The section drawing shows a slender vertical tower with repeated residential floors stacked above a ground-level and basement structure. The section also shows garden balconies repeated along the height of the tower.

The planted balconies are not isolated decorative elements. They are repeated vertically and create a continuous spatial rhythm. The section suggests that garden spaces are lifted upward through the building, making greenery part of every residential level.

The drawing also indicates the building’s height and occupied height, showing the tower as a compact vertical residential structure.

### 2.3 Combined Plan and Section Drawing

The combined drawing helps clarify the relationship between the full-floor apartment plan and the vertical stacking strategy.

The plan shows the horizontal organization of one apartment, while the section shows how this apartment type is repeated vertically. Together, they reveal the main design idea:

**One full-floor garden apartment is repeated vertically to form a high-rise tower.**

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Full-Floor Apartment → occupies → Tower Floor

Each apartment occupies an entire residential floor. This makes every unit more similar to a private house than a typical high-rise apartment module.

### 5.2 Central Living Space → organizes → Residential Rooms

The central living and dining area acts as the main organizing space of the apartment. Bedrooms, service areas, bathrooms, and garden balconies are arranged around it.

### 5.3 Bedroom → connects to → Garden Balcony

Bedrooms are placed near the apartment perimeter and connect visually or physically to planted balcony areas. This allows private rooms to benefit from greenery, views, shade, and privacy.

### 5.4 Living Area → extends to → Garden Balcony

The living area extends outward toward planted balconies, turning the edge of the apartment into a garden-like domestic threshold.

### 5.5 Garden Balcony → filters → City View

The garden balcony creates a planted visual layer between the interior apartment and the surrounding city. This filters views and reduces direct exposure to nearby buildings.

### 5.6 Garden Balcony → shades → Interior Space

The planted balcony and facade depth provide shade for the interior, which is important in Singapore’s tropical climate.

### 5.7 Vertical Core → serves → Full-Floor Apartment

The vertical circulation core provides access to each full-floor apartment. Because there is only one apartment per floor, the lift arrival can feel private and domestic.

### 5.8 Service Zone → supports → Domestic Life

Service spaces such as kitchen, utility, bathrooms, and storage areas are arranged to support the central living space and bedroom zones.

### 5.9 Tall Wall Elements → frame → Garden Balcony

The exterior vertical wall elements frame the planted balconies and create a strong architectural rhythm on the facade.

### 5.10 Repeated Floor Plate → stacks → Garden Homes

The typical full-floor apartment is repeated vertically, creating a stack of private garden homes in the tower.

### 5.11 Roof Garden → caps → Residential Tower

The planted roof or sky garden condition completes the vertical garden concept and reinforces the idea of the tower as a garden residence.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

EDEN’s spatial logic is based on transforming the apartment floor into a private garden home.

The plan does not divide each floor into many small units. Instead, each floor becomes one large residence with multiple orientations, a central living space, and garden balconies around the perimeter.

The main spatial sequence can be summarized as:

**Private Lift Arrival → Central Living Space → Bedrooms / Service Rooms → Garden Balconies → City View**

The vertical logic can be summarized as:

**Full-Floor Garden Apartment → Repeated Vertical Stack → Tropical Residential Tower**

Through this strategy, EDEN combines two different residential ideas:

1. **The privacy and spatial generosity of a detached house**
2. **The density and view advantage of a high-rise tower**

The garden balcony is the key element that connects these two conditions. It allows each apartment to feel like a house with a garden, even though it is stacked vertically in a tower.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that EDEN is not only a luxury residential tower. It is a typological experiment in high-rise domesticity.

The project asks:

**How can a high-rise apartment become more like a private house with a garden?**

The answer is developed through four main strategies:

### 7.1 Full-floor apartment planning

By giving each unit an entire floor, the apartment gains privacy, multiple orientations, and a more house-like spatial organization.

### 7.2 Central domestic space

The large central living and dining area becomes the heart of the apartment. It organizes family life and connects to surrounding rooms and balconies.

### 7.3 Perimeter garden balconies

The planted balconies wrap around parts of the apartment perimeter and create outdoor garden rooms for each residence.

### 7.4 Vertical repetition of garden homes

Each full-floor apartment is repeated vertically, turning the tower into a stack of private garden residences.
