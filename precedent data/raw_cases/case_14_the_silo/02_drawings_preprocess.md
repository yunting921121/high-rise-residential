## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**  
   Important spatial elements, existing concrete structure, residential units, balconies, public functions, facade systems, and adaptive reuse components.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**  
   How the original silo structure, new apartments, vertical core, balconies, faceted facade, public top, and lower-level functions relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**  
   The design logic behind The Silo as a former grain storage structure transformed into residential apartments and public functions.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Residential Floor Plan

The plan shows a long and compact floor plate organized around vertical cores and existing structural walls. Apartment units are inserted into the original silo structure and adapted to the existing geometry.

The plan suggests that residential units are not simply repeated standard apartments. Because the original silo had different structural and spatial conditions, the apartments become varied and unique.

The plan also shows exterior balcony-like extensions around the perimeter. These spaces connect apartments to the outside while maintaining the existing core of the building.

### 2.2 Facade Elevation

The elevation shows a strong faceted facade system. The facade is composed of angular panels, projecting balcony zones, window openings, and folded metal surfaces.

The facade appears as a new outer layer attached to the existing silo. This layer transforms the old industrial volume into a contemporary residential tower while still expressing heaviness, texture, and industrial character.

### 2.3 Exterior Photo

The exterior photo confirms the contrast between the new metallic facade and the surrounding harbor context. The building reads as a silver faceted object on the waterfront, while its underlying mass remains compact and silo-like.

The photo also shows the distinction between the lower levels, the main residential facade, and the transparent top level.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

The Silo’s spatial logic is based on transformation through wrapping.

The adaptive reuse sequence can be summarized as:

**Existing Grain Silo → Preserved Concrete Core → Cut Openings → Inserted Apartments → New Facade Overcoat → Public Top and Base**

The residential sequence can be summarized as:

**Vertical Core → Apartment Unit → Raw Concrete Interior → Floor-to-Ceiling Window / Balcony → Harbor View**

The urban sequence can be summarized as:

**Industrial Harbor Memory → Residential Reuse → Public Viewing Platform → Nordhavn Landmark**

Through this strategy, The Silo combines:

1. **Industrial heritage preservation**
2. **Residential conversion**
3. **Public access**
4. **New climate envelope**
5. **Waterfront landmark identity**
6. **Unique apartment typologies**

The project demonstrates how an obsolete infrastructure building can become a new residential and civic object.

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that The Silo is a project about turning industrial constraint into residential uniqueness.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The old silo is not erased; it is inhabited.**

Instead of treating the existing structure as a problem, the project uses it as the generator of apartment variety. The massive concrete core, unusual floor heights, existing structural walls, and raw material character become the foundation for unique domestic spaces.

The new facade acts as an overcoat. It does not completely deny the industrial past; it upgrades the building and gives it a new urban identity while allowing the interior to retain raw concrete character.
