## 1. Purpose of Drawing Preprocess

This drawing preprocess translates architectural drawings and diagrams into textual information for Precedent DNA analysis.

The purpose is to extract:

1. **Architectural vocabulary**
   Important spatial elements, floor plate types, terrace components, circulation systems, residential layouts, and environmental devices.

2. **Spatial relationship descriptions**
   How slabs, terraces, indoor rooms, vegetation, views, privacy, light, cores, and apartment units relate to each other.

3. **Overall spatial logic**
   The design logic behind Beirut Terraces as a layered high-rise residential tower that creates flexible living between inside and outside.

---

## 2. Drawing Inventory

### 2.1 Five Principles Diagram

The five principles diagram explains the project through five design ideas:

1. Inside and outside
2. Layers and terraces
3. Vegetation and architecture
4. Views and privacy
5. Light and identity

This diagram is important because it shows that the tower is not only a stack of apartments. It is a system of environmental and domestic layers.

### 2.2 Modular Floor Plate Diagrams

The colored floor plate diagrams show that the tower is made of different modular floor types. These modules are repeated in different combinations across the height of the building.

The diagrams suggest that the tower uses repetition, but does not create identical floors. Instead, each level can have different apartment arrangements, terrace shapes, and indoor-outdoor relationships.

### 2.3 Typical Residential Floor Plans

The detailed floor plans show a central core surrounded by apartment units. The units are organized around the perimeter and connected to large terraces. Some terraces are deep and occupy major portions of the floor plate edge.

The plan reveals a strong relationship between interior living spaces and exterior terraces. Living rooms, dining areas, bedrooms, and service spaces are arranged to benefit from light, views, and outdoor space.

### 2.4 Exterior Facade Photo

The exterior photo confirms the drawing logic. The facade is made of horizontal slabs of different depths. Some floors project outward, while others are set back. The result is a stratified tower with terraces, shadow, vegetation, and varied unit expression.

## 5. Spatial Relationship Descriptions

### 5.1 Horizontal Slab → generates → Terrace

Projecting and setback slabs create exterior terrace spaces around the apartments.

### 5.2 Terrace → extends → Living Space

Terraces extend indoor living, dining, and bedroom areas outward, creating a continuous indoor-outdoor residential experience.

### 5.3 Overhang → shades → Interior Space

Large overhangs protect interiors and terraces from direct sunlight, reducing heat gain and creating sheltered outdoor zones.

### 5.4 Vegetation → screens → Apartment Unit

Planting on terraces acts as a privacy screen between apartments and the surrounding urban context.

### 5.5 Terrace → frames → Sea View

The terrace becomes a viewing platform that frames views toward the Mediterranean Sea and Beirut skyline.

### 5.6 Central Core → serves → Apartment Units

The structural and circulation core provides elevators, stairs, services, and vertical organization for the residential tower.

### 5.7 Column Grid → frees → Apartment Layout

A regular structural grid and non-structural interior walls allow apartment layouts to remain flexible.

### 5.8 Modular Floor Type → creates → Unit Variation

Five modular floor types are repeated in different combinations, creating non-identical apartments and varied terrace conditions.

### 5.9 Indoor Space → merges with → Outdoor Terrace

Floor-to-ceiling glazing and terrace continuity blur the boundary between interior rooms and exterior living space.

### 5.10 Layered Facade → balances → Shelter and Exposure

The facade alternates between open terraces, shaded areas, planted screens, and enclosed living spaces.

### 5.11 Quarter Lobby → limits → Shared Circulation

Each quarter of the tower has its own lobby and elevator access, reducing shared circulation and increasing residential privacy.

### 5.12 Light and Shadow → defines → Building Identity

The changing depth of slabs produces strong horizontal shadows and gives the building its layered identity.

## 6. Overall Spatial Logic

Beirut Terraces’ spatial logic is based on layering.

The tower is not designed as a flat facade with standard balconies attached to it. Instead, the whole building is organized as a stack of horizontal platforms. These platforms shift, project, and set back to produce different terrace conditions.

The spatial sequence can be summarized as:

**Core → Apartment Unit → Glazed Interior Edge → Terrace → Vegetation / Shade → City or Sea View**

The vertical logic can be summarized as:

**Five Modular Floor Types → Repeated Combinations → Non-identical Apartments → Layered Tower Identity**

The environmental logic can be summarized as:

**Slab Projection → Shade → Outdoor Comfort → Privacy → Mediterranean Living**

Through this strategy, the building combines:

1. **High-rise residential density**
2. **Private outdoor living**
3. **Flexible apartment layout**
4. **Climate-responsive shading**
5. **View-oriented terraces**
6. **Layered architectural identity**

---

## 7. Design Interpretation

The drawing analysis suggests that Beirut Terraces is a project about transforming the high-rise apartment into a vertical landscape of terraces.

The most important design interpretation is:

**The floor slab becomes the main architectural device.**

The slabs do not only separate floors. They create shade, terraces, privacy, facade depth, and identity. They allow every apartment to have an outdoor relationship with the city and sea.

The tower also challenges the idea of repetitive apartment floors. Although it uses modular floor types, the combinations generate difference. Each unit can have its own terrace condition, degree of exposure, and relationship to view.

This makes the project a useful precedent for high-rise housing where outdoor living, climate response, and privacy are key issues.
