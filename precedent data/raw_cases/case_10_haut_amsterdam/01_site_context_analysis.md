## 1. Urban Location & Positioning

HAUT Amsterdam is located in the Amstelkwartier district of Amsterdam, close to the Amstel River and Park Somerlust. The project is positioned within a new residential development area that transforms former industrial and infrastructural land into a denser urban waterfront neighborhood.

The site can be understood through four main urban conditions:

1. **Riverfront residential development**
   The project is located near the Amstel River, giving the building access to water views, open landscape, and a more relaxed urban edge compared with the inner historic city.

2. **Amstelkwartier transformation area**
   Amstelkwartier is a developing residential district with new apartment buildings, streets, public spaces, and riverfront amenities. HAUT contributes to the district’s new skyline and sustainable identity.

3. **High-rise residential landmark**
   At approximately 73 meters and 21 floors, HAUT functions as a visible residential marker within the Amstelkwartier area.

4. **Sustainable urban prototype**
   The project is not only a residential tower but also a demonstration of sustainable high-rise timber construction in the Netherlands.

HAUT therefore occupies a specific urban role: it connects riverfront living, new district development, timber innovation, and high-rise residential density.

## 4. Sunlight & Shadow Conditions

Amsterdam has a temperate northern European climate with moderate sunlight, frequent cloud cover, wind, and rain. For a residential tower in this climate, daylight, views, insulation, thermal comfort, and sheltered outdoor spaces are important.

HAUT responds through several design strategies:

1. **Large windows**
   The building uses large glazed openings to bring daylight deep into apartments and provide views toward the city, river, and park.

2. **Balconies and terraces**
   The irregular facade includes balconies and terraces that create outdoor living opportunities and add depth to the building envelope.

3. **Flexible floor plans**
   Apartments can be adjusted in layout, allowing different relationships between living spaces, bedrooms, double-height spaces, balconies, and views.

4. **Timber structure and thermal performance**
   The timber-hybrid structural system contributes to a lower-carbon residential tower and creates a warm material identity.

5. **Solar panels and technical systems**
   The roof and facade integrate energy-related systems, supporting the building’s sustainability performance.

Because the building is located close to the river and open landscape, upper floors can receive good daylight and broad views. At the same time, wind exposure and weather protection are important considerations for balconies and terraces.

## 5. Views & Vistas

HAUT’s riverfront location and tower height create several view conditions:

### 5.1 Amstel River View

Apartments can look toward the Amstel River and riverfront public spaces. This gives the building a strong landscape relationship.

### 5.2 Park View

Units facing Park Somerlust can gain views toward trees, green open space, and recreational landscape.

### 5.3 City View

Upper floors can look over Amsterdam’s urban fabric and new development areas.

### 5.4 Terrace View

Balconies and terraces provide direct outdoor viewing spaces, allowing residents to experience the city and river from semi-private exterior rooms.

The view strategy is connected to the building’s irregular facade. Balconies, double-height windows, and varied apartment layouts allow different residential experiences across the tower.

## 6. Design Implications

The site context suggests that HAUT Amsterdam’s design strategy can be interpreted through six main ideas.

### 6.1 Creating a sustainable landmark for Amstelkwartier

HAUT acts as a visible symbol of sustainable urban development in a new riverfront district.

### 6.2 Using timber high-rise construction as urban identity

The project demonstrates that high-rise housing can be built with timber-hybrid construction, reducing dependence on conventional concrete-dominant tower systems.

### 6.3 Connecting residents to river and park views

The tower uses its height and location to provide views toward the Amstel River, Park Somerlust, and Amsterdam.

### 6.4 Creating flexible luxury housing

The project provides apartments with varied layouts, balconies, terraces, and double-height spaces, allowing different domestic arrangements.

### 6.5 Combining sustainable systems with residential comfort

Solar panels, ground-source cooling, low-temperature floor heating / cooling, rainwater storage, and BREEAM-related strategies support environmental performance while maintaining high-end residential quality.

### 6.6 Responding to a new district rather than a historic fabric

The tower contributes to the identity of Amstelkwartier as a contemporary, sustainable, and higher-density residential district.

## 7. Site Context Summary

HAUT Amsterdam is located in Amsterdam’s Amstelkwartier, near the Amstel River and Park Somerlust. It belongs to a new residential district rather than the old canal city.

The main site conditions are:

1. **Riverfront setting**
   The Amstel River provides open views, landscape value, and a strong site identity.

2. **New district development**
   Amstelkwartier is a transforming urban area with new housing, public spaces, and sustainable development ambitions.

3. **Need for high-density housing**
   The tower provides vertical residential density within a growing urban district.

4. **Climate and daylight requirements**
   Amsterdam’s climate requires daylight access, wind-aware balconies, insulation, and comfortable indoor-outdoor thresholds.

5. **Sustainable construction opportunity**
   The project uses timber-hybrid construction to reduce carbon impact and demonstrate a new model for high-rise residential design.

HAUT responds by combining a hybrid timber structure, large windows, balconies, flexible apartments, sustainable technical systems, and riverfront views.

Therefore, HAUT Amsterdam can be understood as a high-rise residential precedent that uses timber construction, sustainable systems, and flexible apartment planning to create a new model of low-carbon urban tower living.
