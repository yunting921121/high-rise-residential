## 1. Urban Location & Positioning

79 & Park is located in Gärdet, Stockholm, at the edge of a large urban landscape connected to the Royal National City Park. The project sits between the open park landscape of Gärdet and the denser residential and urban fabric of Stockholm.

The building can be understood as a housing project positioned between two different conditions:

1. **Park-edge landscape condition**
   The project faces the open landscape of Gärdet, giving the residences access to greenery, long views, and a calmer natural setting.

2. **Urban residential condition**
   The building is also part of Stockholm’s urban fabric, connected to streets, neighboring residential blocks, and district-level infrastructure.

3. **Harbor and city-edge condition**
   The area is also connected to Stockholm’s harbor-related landscape and the wider city edge, creating views toward both nature and urban infrastructure.

4. **Hillside residential identity**
   The building is conceived as an inhabitable landscape or artificial hillside, using stepped massing and planted terraces to mediate between park and city.

79 & Park therefore does not behave like a conventional tower or slab. It is a terraced residential block that transforms the boundary between city and park into an inhabitable topography.

## 4. Sunlight & Shadow Conditions

Stockholm’s northern climate makes daylight access especially important. Winter days are short and sun angles are low, so residential buildings must carefully manage courtyard light, unit orientation, and outdoor space.

79 & Park responds to this condition through massing geometry.

Key sunlight and shadow implications include:

1. **Lowered southwest corner**
   The building steps down at one corner to allow more daylight into the central courtyard and to reduce the sense of enclosure.

2. **Raised northeast corner**
   The opposite corner rises to create a stronger urban landmark and maximize views over the park and surrounding city.

3. **Terraced roof landscape**
   Stepped terraces allow many apartments to have private or shared outdoor spaces with access to sunlight.

4. **Central courtyard daylight**
   The courtyard is shaped by the building’s porous and stepped massing, allowing daylight and views into the interior open space.

5. **Apartment orientation**
   The modular units are arranged around the perimeter and courtyard, creating different orientations, views, and light conditions.

The building therefore uses height variation to balance density, daylight, courtyard quality, and landmark identity.

## 5. Views & Vistas

79 & Park benefits from its position at the edge of Gärdet and its terraced massing.

The project creates several view conditions:

### 5.1 Park View

Many apartments and terraces face Gärdet, giving residents views toward green open space and the Royal National City Park landscape.

### 5.2 Courtyard View

Apartments facing inward look toward the central green courtyard, creating a quieter communal view condition.

### 5.3 City View

Upper-level apartments and roof terraces gain views over Stockholm’s urban fabric and surrounding districts.

### 5.4 Harbor / Infrastructure View

Some upper areas can relate visually to Stockholm’s harbor and wider infrastructural landscape.

The view strategy is closely connected to the stepped form. As the building rises, more units gain broader views. As it lowers toward the park, the building creates a smoother transition between domestic space and landscape.

## 6. Design Implications

The site context suggests that 79 & Park’s design strategy can be interpreted through six main ideas.

### 6.1 Extending the park into housing

The project treats the building as an extension of the park. Roof terraces, planting, courtyards, and wood cladding help blur the boundary between architecture and landscape.

### 6.2 Creating an inhabitable hillside

Instead of creating a flat slab or isolated tower, the building steps upward like a hillside, allowing apartments to occupy a terraced landscape.

### 6.3 Balancing urban density and suburban qualities

The project combines the density of urban housing with qualities associated with suburban homes: private outdoor space, greenery, views, and community.

### 6.4 Maximizing daylight in a courtyard block

The massing is lowered in one direction to bring light into the courtyard and raised in another direction to provide views and urban presence.

### 6.5 Using modularity to create variation

The building is composed of repeated square modules, but their stacking creates varied apartments, terraces, facade depths, and roof conditions.

### 6.6 Creating a communal courtyard

The central courtyard becomes a shared green space for residents and acts as the social heart of the block.

## 7. Site Context Summary

79 & Park is located at Sandhamnsgatan 79 in Stockholm’s Gärdet district, at the edge of a large park landscape connected to the Royal National City Park.

The main site conditions are:

1. **Park-edge location**
   The project sits beside a large urban green landscape.

2. **Urban residential density**
   The building still belongs to Stockholm’s residential fabric and must provide compact urban housing.

3. **Need for daylight**
   Stockholm’s northern daylight condition requires careful massing and courtyard design.

4. **Need for outdoor space**
   The project uses terraces and roof gardens to give apartments access to private and shared outdoor space.

5. **Need for contextual transition**
   The building mediates between city, park, harbor, and residential neighborhood.

79 & Park responds by transforming the residential block into a terraced hillside. Its stepped massing, central courtyard, modular units, wood facade, and roof landscape create a housing model that is both urban and landscape-oriented.

Therefore, 79 & Park can be understood as a high-rise / mid-rise residential precedent that uses terraced massing, modular planning, and park-edge landscape integration to combine density with domestic outdoor living.
