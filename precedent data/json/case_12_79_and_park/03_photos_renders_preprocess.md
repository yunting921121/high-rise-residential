## 1. Purpose of Photos and Renders Preprocess

This preprocess translates photos, renders, and visual materials into textual descriptions for architectural precedent analysis.

The goal is to extract:

1. **Design vocabulary**
   Visible architectural elements, terraced roof landscape, wood facade, central courtyard, modular units, and park-edge conditions.

2. **Atmospheric description**
   The visual and spatial atmosphere created by the building’s hillside form, timber material, planted terraces, and relationship to Gärdet.

3. **Design intent reading**
   The possible design intention behind the transformation of a residential block into an inhabitable landscape.

4. **Contextual relationship**
   How the building relates to the park, Stockholm’s residential fabric, daylight, views, and domestic outdoor living.

## 3. Atmospheric Description

79 & Park creates the atmosphere of a residential block transformed into a hillside landscape.

From the exterior image, the building appears to rise gradually from the park edge. The stepped massing, roof terraces, planted surfaces, and wood facade make the project feel softer and more landscape-like than a conventional urban block.

The atmosphere can be described through five qualities:

### 3.1 Park-Edge Domesticity

The building feels connected to the park. Its terraces and roof gardens extend the landscape into the residential block.

### 3.2 Pixelated Human Scale

Although the building is large, its modular composition breaks the mass into smaller domestic units. This gives the project a more human scale.

### 3.3 Inhabitable Roofscape

The roof is not a leftover technical surface. It becomes a series of outdoor rooms, gardens, terraces, and viewpoints.

### 3.4 Courtyard Community

The central courtyard creates a shared internal landscape. It gives the building a communal center and organizes residential life around green space.

### 3.5 Soft Urban Landmark

The raised corner gives the building landmark presence, while the wood facade and greenery soften its urban scale.

## 5. Light and Environmental Experience

79 & Park’s environmental experience is shaped by its stepped courtyard massing.

The environmental logic includes:

1. **Lowered corner improves daylight.**
   The lower southwest corner allows more sunlight to reach the courtyard.

2. **Raised corner improves views.**
   The taller corner provides more panoramic views toward the park and city.

3. **Terraces create outdoor living.**
   Many units have direct access to private or shared terraces.

4. **Courtyard creates protected landscape.**
   The inner courtyard gives residents a quieter communal outdoor environment.

5. **Wood and vegetation soften climate experience.**
   Natural materials and planting create a warmer, more domestic atmosphere.

The project uses massing as a daylight and landscape strategy rather than treating form as pure visual expression.

## 6. Spatial Hierarchy

The photos and drawings suggest a clear spatial hierarchy:

### 6.1 Park Layer

The building is located at the edge of Gärdet and visually extends the park landscape.

### 6.2 Urban Block Layer

The building still functions as a dense residential block within Stockholm’s urban fabric.

### 6.3 Courtyard Layer

The central courtyard provides shared green space and organizes community life.

### 6.4 Apartment Layer

Apartments are arranged around the perimeter and courtyard, with different view and terrace conditions.

### 6.5 Terrace / Roof Landscape Layer

The stepped roof creates outdoor spaces, gardens, and views across the building.

### 6.6 Landmark Layer

The raised corner creates a stronger urban presence and skyline identity.

The hierarchy can be summarized as:

**Park → Residential Block → Central Courtyard → Apartment Units → Terraced Roof Landscape → Urban Landmark**

## 7. Design Intent Reading

From the exterior photo, plan, section, and facade drawings, 79 & Park can be interpreted as a project that redefines the residential block as a landscape.

The design intent can be understood in six main ways:

### 7.1 Combining suburban and urban living

The project gives residents access to gardens, terraces, and park views while maintaining urban density.

### 7.2 Turning the roof into landscape

The stepped roof becomes a series of inhabitable terraces and green surfaces.

### 7.3 Creating a social courtyard

The central courtyard provides a shared landscape that supports community and visual connection.

### 7.4 Using modularity without monotony

The building uses repeated modules but arranges them into a varied and pixelated massing.

### 7.5 Responding to daylight

The lowered corner brings more light into the courtyard, while the raised corner creates views and identity.

### 7.6 Softening the urban block

Wood cladding, greenery, and terraced form soften the scale of a large residential block.

---

## 8. Contextual Relationship

79 & Park relates to its context in four main ways:

### 8.1 Relationship with Gärdet

The building is conceived as an extension of the park, with roof terraces and vegetation continuing the landscape upward.

### 8.2 Relationship with Stockholm’s urban fabric

The project provides urban residential density while maintaining a domestic and landscape-oriented character.

### 8.3 Relationship with daylight and climate

The stepped form is shaped to bring daylight into the courtyard and provide outdoor spaces in a northern climate.

### 8.4 Relationship with residential community

The courtyard and terraces create shared and private outdoor spaces that support communal intimacy.

## 10. Preprocess Output Summary

### Main Visual Character

79 & Park presents a residential courtyard block as an inhabitable hillside. Its identity is defined by stepped massing, modular units, roof terraces, wood cladding, and its relationship to Gärdet.

### Main Atmospheric Quality

The project creates an atmosphere of park-edge domesticity. It is dense and urban, but also green, terraced, communal, and landscape-oriented.

### Main Design Reading

79 & Park uses a modular courtyard block to combine urban density with suburban qualities such as gardens, terraces, park views, and shared landscape. Its key contribution is the transformation of a housing block into a terraced residential landscape.
